Van Eynde, Frank & Kim, Jong-Bok: Pseudo-partitives in English

Pseudo-partitives are [N1–of–N2] sequences in which N1 denotes a quantity or measure and in which N2 is a bare plural or a singular mass noun, as in a lot of things, a liter of water and a group of volunteers. In terms of agreement, there are two types of such sequences: those which share the number value of N1, and those which share the number value of N2. The analysis of the first type is straightforward, but the second type raises issues about the status of N1 and the preposition of. We show how they can be dealt with if one adopts the functor treatment of the noun phrase.
