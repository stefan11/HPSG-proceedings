Nagaya, Naonori: Affectedness and volitionality: the case of Tagalog

In this paper, I argue that the manifestation of affectedness is negatively correlated with that of volitionality in Tagalog, a language of the Philippines. It is shown that volitional verbs do not necessarily entail an affected patient, while non-volitional verbs always indicate an affected patient. The cross-linguistically attested pattern of prototypical transitive constructions, in which a volitional agent acts on and affects a patient, is not found in this language. Thus, this study sheds new light on our understanding of affectedness and more generally, theories of transitivity.
