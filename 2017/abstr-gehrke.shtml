Gehrke, Berit: On GOOD manners

This paper elaborates and qualifies McNelly and Kennedy’s (2005, 2013) claim that ‘degree’ WELL is an event modifier by examining its distribution in English and German. Both manner and ‘degree’ readings of WELL share a common semantic core: good is applied to an event. The ‘degree’ reading arises when WELL applies to a (result) state of a non-agentive event that selects a highly affected argument.
